About Dr. Kalam

Biography

1931 - 2015

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Rising from humble beginnings, he became one of India's most celebrated scientists and the 11th President of India (2002-2007).

Known as the "People's President," Dr. Kalam dedicated his life to science, education, and inspiring the youth of India. His contributions to India's missile and nuclear programs earned him the title "Missile Man of India."

Key Achievements

SLV-III Project

Led India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle project, making India a space-faring nation in 1980.

Pokhran-II Tests

Played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, establishing India as a nuclear power.

11th President

Served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007), known as the "People's President" for his accessibility.

Life Journey

Timeline

October 15, 1931

Birth

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a Tamil Muslim family. His father was a boat owner and imam.

1958

Joins DRDO

Started his career as a scientist at DRDO after graduating in Aerospace Engineering from MIT, Chennai.

1969

Transfers to ISRO

Moved to ISRO where he served as the project director of India's first SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle).

July 18, 1980

SLV-III Success

Successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into orbit, making India the fourth nation with launch capability.

1997

Bharat Ratna

Awarded India's highest civilian honor for his immense contributions to science and technology.

2002-2007

11th President of India

Elected as President with overwhelming majority. Known for his vision of transforming India into a developed nation by 2020.

July 27, 2015

A Star Returns Home

Passed away while doing what he loved most — delivering a lecture to students at IIM Shillong. He was 83.
